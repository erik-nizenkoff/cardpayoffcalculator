Methodology

The calculator applies monthly interest using APR divided by 12, then pays each card's entered minimum before applying the extra monthly payment. Avalanche targets the highest APR balance. Snowball targets the smallest balance. Custom follows the card order shown in the table. Fixed payment keeps the entered minimums and splits extra payments across active cards.

Results are estimates. Issuer minimum formulas, fees, penalty APRs, new charges, promotional rates, payment timing, and compounding rules can change actual payoff dates and interest.

FAQ

Why does the calculator warn about minimum payments?

If a minimum payment barely covers monthly interest, the balance can take decades to fall. Extra principal payments are usually needed to make meaningful progress.

Should I choose avalanche or snowball?

Choose avalanche to reduce interest. Choose snowball if closing smaller accounts sooner will help you keep going.

Does this store my debt information?

No. The current prototype runs calculations in the browser and does not send or store your numbers.
